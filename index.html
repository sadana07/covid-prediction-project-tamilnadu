COVID-19 Awareness

COVID-19 is an infectious respiratory disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which triggered a global pandemic after emerging in late 2019. It spreads primarily through respiratory droplets or aerosols, causing symptoms ranging from mild (fever, cough, fatigue) to severe pneumonia or death. Vaccination, masking, and social distancing are key prevention measures, according to the World Health Organization (WHO).

Key Aspects of COVID-19:

Cause: Caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which primarily attacks the respiratory system, as outlined in the Wikipedia article on COVID-19.

Symptoms: Common symptoms include fever, cough, fatigue, and loss of taste or smell.

Transmission: Spreads from an infected person's mouth or nose through respiratory droplets or smaller aerosols when they cough, sneeze, speak, or breathe.

Prevention: Vaccination is the most effective protection, along with handwashing, wearing masks, and improving ventilation.

High Risk: Older adults and those with underlying medical conditions like diabetes or heart disease are at a higher risk of severe illness.
